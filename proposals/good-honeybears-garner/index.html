Where the opportunity lives

Four issues a parent sees in seconds.

After a close review of your current site, we identified four specific problems actively undermining what you've built over 25 years.

01

Placeholder Blog Content Is Still Live

Your blog section contains articles filled with Lorem ipsum dummy text — the default placeholder copy that shows up before real content is added. These articles are publicly visible to any parent who finds your site. When a parent scrolling your blog sees "Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet," they don't know it's a mistake. They think the site is either fake, broken, or hasn't been touched since it launched. That impression spreads.

02

No Online Enrollment Path

A parent who decides they want to learn more about Good-HoneyBears at 9pm on a Tuesday can't do anything about it. There's no "Request a Tour" button, no enrollment inquiry form, no way to take action other than calling during business hours. In 2026, that friction alone is sending interested families to your competitors who make it easy to take that first step digitally.

03

Your 25-Year History Is Invisible

The most compelling thing about Good-HoneyBears — a 25-year track record in Garner — is not prominent anywhere on the site. Parents searching for stable, established childcare care deeply about longevity. A center that has been operating for 25 years is statistically far more reliable than a franchise that opened two years ago. That story should be the first thing parents read. Right now, it isn't told at all.

04

No Google Business or Reviews Integration

When parents find you through Google, they see your Google Business listing with reviews. When they click through to your website, those reviews disappear — there's no integration bringing social proof onto your own pages. Parents who need one more reason to call you can't find it on your site. A simple reviews section could be the difference between an inquiry and a missed enrollment.
