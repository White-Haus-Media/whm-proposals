Where the opportunity lives

Your online presence has room to work harder for you.

After reviewing your current website, we identified four areas where a stronger digital presence would directly impact how many new families choose Bright Beginnings.

01

No Online Enrollment or Waitlist

The current site has no way for parents to start the enrollment process or join a waitlist online. In zip code 27519 — where 78% of adults hold a bachelor's degree and the median household income is $165,000 — parents expect to start the process digitally. Right now, the only path is calling during business hours or sending an email to a general inbox.

02

Staff Page Is Empty

The staff page exists in the navigation but contains no teacher bios, photos, or credentials. For a center built on the Herndon family's teaching legacy and low staff turnover, this is the single biggest missed opportunity on the site. Parents want to see who will be caring for their child — especially at a 5-star rated center where the team is a core differentiator.

03

No Social Proof on the Website

Google reviews reference families who have been with Bright Beginnings for years, calling the staff "like family" and praising the 5-star rating. None of this appears on the website itself. The testimonials page exists but is disconnected from the rest of the site — there is no review feed, no rating badge, and no parent quotes on the homepage or programs pages where they would matter most.

04

SEO Fundamentals Are Missing

The site has no H1 heading tags on any page, missing alt text on images, and no structured data markup for Google. For searches like "child care Cary NC" or "5-star daycare 27519," these are the basics that determine whether Bright Beginnings appears in results. The blog has one post in five years, which signals to search engines that the site is inactive.
