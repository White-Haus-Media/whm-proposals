Our Story

Founded by educators. Run by family.

In May 2000, Shirley Herndon — a retired Wake County educator with 32 years in the classroom — and her husband Leon, with 38 years in education, opened Bright Beginnings on their family's Cary property. Their motivation was simple: the most successful students they taught had early exposure to varied learning opportunities.

Today, the center is still a family operation. Daughter Lisa Ware serves as Assistant Director, and son Linwood Herndon leads marketing. All of the Herndons' grandchildren have attended the center. The family legacy traces back to Bertha Scott Herndon, Leon's mother, who taught elementary education in Chatham County for 38 years and inspired the center's creation.

Programs

Care designed for every stage

From their first months through kindergarten readiness, each program follows the Creative Curriculum and is built around where your child is developmentally.

3–12 Months · 1:4 Ratio

Infant Care

Nurturing, one-on-one attention in a safe and responsive environment. Individualized schedules for feeding, napping, and play that follow each baby's natural rhythm and developmental milestones.

12–24 Months · 1:5 Ratio

Toddler Program

Hands-on exploration and early social development. Language-rich activities that build vocabulary, spark curiosity, and grow confidence through guided play.

24–36 Months · 1:8 Ratio

Preschool I

Building independence through structured routines, creative expression, and early literacy foundations.

3 Years Old · 1:9 Ratio

Preschool II

Deepening social skills, letter recognition, counting, and cooperative play through the Creative Curriculum framework.

4–5 Years Old · 1:12 Ratio

KinderPrep

Comprehensive kindergarten readiness — reading foundations, math concepts, writing skills, and the social confidence to succeed in elementary school.

Five stars from the State of North Carolina — the highest possible rating for a child care center. A clean March 2026 inspection confirms the standard is maintained every single day.
