Our Story

Three generations of educators.

A family legacy built on the belief that love and learning go hand in hand.

Bright Beginnings was founded in May 2000 by Shirley and Leon Herndon, both retired Wake County educators with over 60 years of combined classroom experience. Shirley served 32 years as a teacher and assistant principal. Leon spent 38 years in education as a teacher, assistant principal, and principal.

The center was built on property belonging to Bertha Scott Herndon, Leon's mother, who taught elementary education in Chatham County Public Schools for 38 years. Her legacy of dedicated teaching inspired three of her children to follow her into education. Today, the Herndon family tradition continues with daughter Lisa Ware as Assistant Director and son Linwood Herndon as Marketing Director.

The Creative Curriculum

Young children learn best by doing.

Our curriculum is built on five fundamental beliefs that guide everything we do in the classroom.

The Creative Curriculum emphasizes that learning requires active thinking and experimenting. Through constructive, purposeful play, children develop across all domains: cognitive, social-emotional, physical, and language.

Our teachers create environments where relationships form the foundation for learning, assessment and curriculum work hand in hand, and family partnerships are central to every child's success.

Four Areas of Development

Cognitive

Problem-solving, critical thinking, and curiosity

Social-Emotional

Empathy, cooperation, and self-regulation

Physical

Motor skills, coordination, and body awareness

Language

Communication, vocabulary, and early literacy
