Where the opportunity lives

Four things a Wake Forest parent doesn't see on your current site.

After a close review of bridgesandbeyond.org this week, here's what we identified as the four highest-impact issues.

I.

Domain Split Kills Trust and Deliverability

Site lives at bridgesandbeyond.org, but email (debi@bridgesandbeyond.com) and footer use a different .com variant. Parents checking the domain see inconsistency; SPF/DKIM suffers, making emails more likely to hit spam.

II.

No Online Enrollment, Waitlist, or Tour Booking

Every inquiry routes through email or phone. A parent ready to act at 9pm on Sunday has no way to move forward. By Monday morning, they've already reached out to two other preschools.

III.

8:1 Ratio + Inclusion Buried, Not Merchandised

The real differentiator — max 8 children per class, inclusion of children with special needs — is not on the hero or in the nav. It's the reason you built this program, and it's invisible.

IV.

Aging Divi Theme on WordPress

Running an old Divi version. Parents recognize dated design instantly; it signals a program that may not be keeping up. Your program absolutely is. The site just isn't showing it.
