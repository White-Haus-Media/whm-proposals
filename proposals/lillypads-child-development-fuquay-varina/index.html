Where the opportunity lives

Four reasons your website is turning families away.

After a thorough review of your current site and digital presence, we found four specific issues actively working against your enrollment growth.

01

A Website Built for 2005, Not 2026

Your site uses image-based navigation — meaning parents on mobile phones (the majority of searchers) can't reliably use your menu. The layout hasn't been updated in approximately 15 years. Before a parent reads a word about your programs or your Four-Star license, the dated design tells them a story about your center that isn't accurate. In a market where parents compare 5–6 centers before calling, design is the first filter.

02

No Way for Parents to Take Action

There's no enrollment form, no tour request button, no "Contact Us" mechanism that actually works. Parents who decide they're interested in Lillypads at 9pm while scrolling on their phones have no way to act on that decision. By morning, they've scheduled a tour at a competitor. Your phone number is listed, but parents — especially parents of young children — don't call businesses cold. They fill out forms.

03

Your Four-Star License Is Invisible

North Carolina's Four-Star childcare license is a significant achievement that puts you above most of your competition. It should be the first thing parents see when they visit your site. Right now it's mentioned nowhere on your homepage. Parents who don't already know what a Four-Star license means — and most don't — will never find out from your website that you hold one.

04

Competing with Fully Funded Chains

Fuquay-Varina now has KinderCare, Primrose School, The Goddard School, and Kiddie Academy — all with professionally maintained websites, Google Ads budgets, and active social media. Parents searching for "daycare Fuquay-Varina" see them first, with polished sites that build trust instantly. Lillypads' 17-year track record is genuinely competitive with any of them — but only if your online presence says so.
